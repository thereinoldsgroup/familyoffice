Infrastructure & compliance

Security is not a feature. It's the foundation.

Your clients trust you with their most sensitive financial data. We build Clarus accordingly.

🔐
AES-256 Encryption at Rest
All data stored in Supabase PostgreSQL is encrypted at rest using AES-256. Sensitive fields — API keys, tokens, credentials — are additionally encrypted at the application layer before storage.
🔒
TLS Encryption in Transit
All data in motion is protected by TLS 1.3. There is no path between your browser and the database that is not encrypted end-to-end.
👁
Role-Based Access Control
Granular permissions down to the module and action level. Every user has a defined role; every role has defined permissions. No one sees data they shouldn't see.
📋
Immutable Audit Logs
Every data access, modification, and authentication event is logged with a timestamp, user identity, and change record. Logs are retained for 7 years in compliance with SOX Section 802.
⏱
Automated Backups & Recovery
Daily automated backups with Point-in-Time Recovery (PITR). Database can be restored to any moment within the retention window. DR procedures are documented and tested quarterly.
🛡
Multi-Factor Authentication
MFA is available for all users and enforceable as a platform-wide policy for Enterprise accounts. Session timeouts and login attempt limits are configurable per organization.

Founder's note

Why we built Clarus

We managed the books for a family office with seven entities across Florida and Georgia — two operating businesses, two real estate holdings, a family trust, a charitable foundation, and a management company. The books lived in four separate QuickBooks files, a stack of Excel consolidation sheets, and a folder of PDF K-1s that needed to be retyped into TurboTax every March.

We looked at the enterprise alternatives. Sage Intacct quoted us $45,000 to implement. NetSuite wanted $2,800 a month before a single add-on. Neither was designed for the nuances of family office accounting — trust structures, intercompany management fees, trust structures, and entity-level reporting.

So we built the system we needed. Clarus is the financial operating system for organizations where complexity is the default, not the exception — and where the people managing it deserve tools that actually work.
